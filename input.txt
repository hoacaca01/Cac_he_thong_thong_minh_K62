Quang Binh's topography is narrow and steep from the west to the east. 85% of the total natural area is hilly. The entire area is divided into basic ecological zones: high mountains, hills and midlands, plains, coastal sandy areas.

The topography is mainly narrow and steep, inclined from west to east, hills and mountains occupy 85% of the province's area and is strongly dissected. Almost the entire western region of the province is mountainous with the height of 1,000-1,500 m, of which the highest is Phi Co Phi peak at 2017 m, followed by low hills, distributed in an upside-down bowl pattern. Near the coast there is a small and narrow plain. Finally, the coastal sands are crescent-shaped or fan-shaped.
